Catalog Description: This course will develop mobile apps that are used to process screen data, and send the data to a server connected to a relational database such as MySQL. Software engineering principles will be used in the development process. Techniques to verify user screen input will be examined.

Prerequisites: engineering standing, and previous iOS experience approved by the instructor


Required course: elective


 Outcomes of Instruction: Students will improve their abilities, knowledge, understanding and skills in these areas:

  1. Design and implement mobile apps that can communicate to a server and database

  2. Use software engineering principles for project development

  3. Understand techniques used to verify user data entered on mobile apps


List of Topics Covered:

  1. iOS mobile app development

  2. Communication of data from a mobile app to a server database

  3. Software engineering principles used for project development

  4. Techniques for verifying user data entered into a mobile app

Individual assignments (50% of grade)


Each student will be given individual programming assignments to complete that will be part of the completed project. Following software engineering procedures, the components (for example, design, test plan, code) of each individual assignment are reviewed in review meetings by all the students and the instructor. The instructor grades individual assignments:

  1. Submission on time (50% penalty for late assignments, zero after the late submission period for the assignment)

  2. Acceptable/unacceptable rating by the instructor. “Acceptable”: Meets the criteria of software development, that will be documented and discussed in the class. These criteria will be typical industry software standards that are studied in CS 498, and applied in CS 499. “Unacceptable”: 10% penalty for first unacceptable assignment, 25% second, 50% for all others.

So that students understand the criteria, the first assignment of each category (for example, design, testing, coding) will only be graded for item 1.


Project outcome (20% of grade)


The students will work together, to develop a mobile app to access a server database. Based on the instructor's rating of the effort of the students, the instructor will grade the project outcome:

20%: Instructor rates the project a success, or if not, instructor rates it a success based on student effort, and problems faced

10%: Project is a partial success, but has a major flaw that should have been overcome

0%: Project failure due to insufficient effort, or lack of following instructor directions

If a student does not participate on the project, as judged by the other students or the instructor, the student's grade for the outcome will be reduced by the instructor.


Project participation (10% of grade)


Students work together on the project, and are expected to meet as needed outside of class to discuss the project. Student meetings are subject to student availability. No student will be expected to attend a meeting that conflicts with the student's availability. Students are expected to notify the other students and the instructor when they cannot attend a scheduled meeting.


Assignments  (10% of grade)

There will be a few written assignments during the semester to be done individually.

 Attendance  (10% of grade)

There will be up to two weekly meetings with the instructor. Attendance is required for all students. Of the 10% attendance grade, 2% is subtracted for each nonattendance, without an excused absence. For example, A student missing 2 meetings, the student gets 6% of the 10% attendance grade. Instructor meetings will be held when the instructor deems them necessary for the team project. There may be weeks with only one meeting, or no meeting at all. Students still work on their individual parts of the project every week.

University attendance policy:

 Students can be excused for University accepted 1) serious illness; 2) illness or death of family member; 3) University-related trips (S.R. 5.2.4.2.C); 4) major religious holidays; 5) other circumstances that the instructor finds to be "reasonable cause for nonattendance."  It is the student’s responsibility to contact the instructor regarding the nature of the absence (within 10 days of the absence), and the instructor retains the right to ask for proof.

Students anticipating an absence for a major religious holiday are responsible for notifying the instructor in writing of anticipated absences due to their observance of such holidays no later than the last day in the semester to add a class. Information regarding dates of major religious holidays may be obtained through the religious liaison, Mr. Jake Karnes (859-257-2754).

The instructor realizes that students often have personal or employment reasons for not being available for a required instructor or student meetings. Send the instructor email when this occurs, and for any valid reason, the instructor will give you an excused absence.

Incomplete grade: Because of the team project nature of the class, the grade of incomplete (I) will be given only in cases of extreme hardship in accordance with the University policy.

 Late Policy: Assignment dates must be met or points will be deducted from the grade..  Assignments will have due dates with penalties for late submission.

 Academic Honor Code: Group projects allow the sharing of ideas and computer code within the group. The University of Kentucky’s guidelines regarding academic dishonesty will be strictly enforced. Note that the penalty for plagiarism can be a failing grade in the course. You must credit sources for any code, algorithms, ideas, etc., in your documentation. If you are in doubt whether you are violating plagiarism guidelines, check with the instructor.

Accommodation: If you have a documented disability that requires academic accommodations, please contact the instructor as soon as possible. In order to receive accommodations in this course, you must provide a Letter of Accommodation from the Disability Resource Center.
